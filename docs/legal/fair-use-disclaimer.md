### What "Fair Use" Means for You

U.S. copyright law recognizes that using copyrighted material in a **transformative** way — to create something new rather than to copy the original — is protected under fair use (17 U.S.C. 107). When you use our generation tools:

- **Your outputs are transformative.** The platform uses reference material as a creative guide, not as a reproduction source. What you generate is new content informed by existing material, not a copy of it.
- **You are the publisher.** When you download, share, or distribute content you've created on this platform, you take on the rights and responsibilities of a publisher — the same way you would if you wrote a blog post, recorded a podcast, or edited a video.
- **Fair use is a legal defense, not a blanket permission.** It's evaluated on a case-by-case basis considering: (1) the purpose and character of the use, (2) the nature of the copyrighted work, (3) how much was used, and (4) the effect on the market for the original. Transformative, non-reproductive use — which is what our tools produce — has the strongest fair use standing.

### If You Receive a Copyright Claim

If a platform (YouTube, etc.) or a rights holder issues a copyright claim against content you created using our tools:

1. **Read the claim carefully.** Most claims are automated and many are resolved by simply disputing them with an explanation of fair use.
2. **You may dispute the claim** under fair use if your content is transformative. Many creators do this successfully.
3. **We are NOT a party to the dispute.** We provided the generation tool; you made the creative decisions and published the content. The claim is between you and the claimant.
4. **We will not remove your account** for receiving external copyright claims. That is between you and the external platform. Our own content policies (see Content Policy) are separate.

## DMCA Takedown Process

If you are a copyright holder and believe content on our platform infringes your rights, submit a DMCA takedown notice to:

**DMCA Agent:** legal@foai.cloud

Your notice must include:
1. Identification of the copyrighted work
2. Identification of the infringing material with sufficient detail to locate it
3. Your contact information
4. A statement of good faith belief that the use is not authorized
5. A statement under penalty of perjury that the information is accurate
6. Your physical or electronic signature

We will process valid DMCA takedown notices in accordance with 17 U.S.C. 512.

## Counter-Notification

If your content was removed and you believe it was removed in error or qualifies as fair use, you may file a counter-notification to legal@foai.cloud with:

1. Identification of the material that was removed
2. A statement under penalty of perjury that you believe the material was removed in error
3. Your name, address, and phone number
4. A statement consenting to jurisdiction of your local federal district court
