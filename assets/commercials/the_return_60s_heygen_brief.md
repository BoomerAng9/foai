# Commercial Brief — "The Return" (60s)

**Platform:** FOAI umbrella, with one scene each for The Deploy Platform, Per|Form, AIMS.
**Primary renderer:** HeyGen v4 for ACHEEVY and any human analysts (per `project_heygen_v4_identity_layer_2026_04_16.md` — one reference video = consistent subject across all scenes).
**Secondary renderer:** Seedance / Veo / Kling for non-character environmental shots, composited in post.
**Runtime:** 60 seconds (can cut down to 30s or extend to 90s; storyboard is modular).
**Audience:** prospective users, partners, press — first impression of the ecosystem.
**Tone:** grounded sci-fi, coastal Georgia warmth, cinematic. Not dystopian. Not militaristic. Not mystical.

## Governing canon (non-negotiable)

- `project_foai_universe_canon.md` — character visual rules, setting, typography, bird species
- `feedback_spinner_is_the_armament.md` — NO firearms / blades / cyber-weapons visible anywhere
- `feedback_names_are_intentional.md` — every platform scene must earn its name literally
- `assets/brand/acheevy/image_brief_acheevy_spinner_bandolier.md` — ACHEEVY character spec
- `feedback_boomerang_spinner_image.md` — canonical Spinner visual (black leather-wrapped boomerang, engraved "ANG" metal band, gold inner-edge glow)

## Story beats (7 beats, 60 seconds)

### Beat 1 — Establishing (0:00 – 0:07)

**Location:** FOAI port, coastal Georgia, golden hour. AIMS shipping containers line the dock. Pink/coral/orange sky. A wood stork wings across the distance. (Not pelicans.)

**Subject:** ACHEEVY stands at the dock edge, three-quarter rear angle. Tan leather jacket, cargo pants, tactical gloves, full helmet with glowing orange-gold visor. 2-chevron rank above helmet. Across his chest: a leather bandolier holding **6 Spinners** (black leather-wrapped boomerangs, engraved ANG bands, gold inner-edge glow).

**Action:** Wide establishing shot. Ambient wind + distant wave lapping + faint wood stork call. Camera slowly pushes in from 20m out to medium-shot over 5 seconds. ACHEEVY raises his right gloved hand to the bandolier, draws one Spinner.

**Audio bed starts:** low warm synth pad + single low drone. Think *Blade Runner 2049* restraint, not Zimmer BRAAM.

**Copy:** none. Visual only.

### Beat 2 — The Throw (0:07 – 0:09)

**Action:** ACHEEVY's arm extends in one clean horizontal motion. Spinner releases. Camera whip-pans to follow — the Spinner rockets frame-right, leaving a thin gold trail like a light-painting afterimage. Whoosh on the release, slightly compressed low-end (the Spinner has weight).

**Transition out:** the Spinner crosses the frame edge; match-cut to Beat 3 on the whoosh.

### Beat 3 — FOAI scene (0:09 – 0:17)

**Thesis earned:** *FOAI = Future of AI* — show AI doing something that existing AI can't quite do yet. This is the umbrella scene.

**Visual:** interior of a darkened command center. Multiple large displays on all walls showing half-complete thoughts: a research paper with missing sections, a strategic plan with question marks, a half-built agent definition. Subtle particle motion around each display (AI "reasoning" visualization, minimal, not dramatic).

**Spinner passes through** (frame-right → frame-left, 1-second pass). In its wake, each display fills in its own missing sections — not with text appearing character-by-character (that's derivative) but with **structural completion**: the paper's missing section headers become connected thought-graphs that self-organize; the strategic plan's question marks become probability-weighted branches that pick themselves; the agent definition self-defines its tool-registry entries.

**Copy (on-screen, subtle, Outfit typeface):** *"Reasoning, completed."*

**Sound:** the whoosh persists underneath; displays make soft chime as each resolves.

### Beat 4 — The Deploy Platform scene (0:17 – 0:26)

**Thesis earned:** *literally deploys services, apps, agents.*

**Visual:** exterior shift — the AIMS container yard on the dock. Some containers are dark, lids chained shut (dormant services). Some are open, empty of contents.

**Spinner passes through**. As it flies between containers, each one lights up from the inside:
- First container: royal-blue + gold glow through the seams, a miniature website materializes on a holographic projection above the lid — domain name visible, actually finishes loading.
- Second container: an agent silhouette (Boomer_Ang with Spinner in hip holster) steps out, ANG patch on chest, no weapons — just deployed.
- Third container: a Docker-style stack of running services, health indicators going from red to green one-by-one.
- Wood stork lifts off from one of the container tops.

**Copy:** *"Services, apps, agents — deployed."*

**Sound:** each container's ignition hits a rising tonal chord.

### Beat 5 — Per|Form scene (0:26 – 0:37)

**Thesis earned:** *both sides of the pipe — Performance AND Form.* This is the longest scene by design because it has to do double work. It's also where most sports-platform commercials skip the paperwork half.

**Visual:** interior — locker-room table, a young college athlete seated (HeyGen-generated figure, any ethnicity, late teens/early 20s, focused and calm). In front of them, two piles of work, one on each side of a literal dividing line on the table (echoing the `|` in the logo):

**Left pile (Per / Performance side):**
- A draft scouting report with empty grade fields
- A TIE partition sheet with missing pillar breakdowns (Body / Mind / Character)
- A historical comp forecast with the longevity curve blank
- Film timestamps with blank annotation boxes

**Right pile (Form / Paperwork side):**
- An NIL endorsement contract with clauses highlighted red (undisclosed terms, non-market rates)
- An eligibility waiver with missing attestation boxes
- A transferable-credit transcript with unreviewed line items
- A health / medical record with blank insurance fields

**Spinner passes through** the center line of the table. In its wake, **both piles resolve**:
- Scouting report auto-grades with pillar breakdowns visible
- TIE partition fills with numerical scores
- Longevity forecast curves to a projection
- Film annotations appear in cadence
- Red clauses flip to green ("AI-reviewed: market rate ± verified")
- Eligibility boxes self-attest with chain-of-custody trail
- Transcript line items verify
- Health record populates with insurance + consent history

The athlete looks up from the table, exhales, picks up a pen — confident enough to sign on their own terms now.

**Copy:** *"Performance and paperwork, handled."*

**Sound:** soft paper-shuffle + a clean single pen-click at the end.

### Beat 6 — AIMS scene (0:37 – 0:47)

**Thesis earned:** *AI Managed Solutions* — AI is the operator, not the assistant. The DevOps team is the Hawks.

**Visual:** a wide-angle operations room view — think a modern NOC / SOC, multiple monitors on a wall, but the "operators" at each workstation are Lil_Hawks (small, skittering, quick). Each Lil_Hawk tends one monitor:
- One monitor shows a Cloud Run service auto-scaling in real time (graph rising, pods spinning up)
- One shows Neon database migrations running cleanly
- One shows a spawned container being health-checked, green-green-green
- One shows a failed deploy auto-rolling back and re-provisioning

**Spinner passes through** the room from entrance to exit. The Lil_Hawks each pause for a beat as the Spinner passes — their monitors resolve whatever's pending — then continue tending.

**Copy:** *"Solutions, managed."*

**Sound:** keyboards + light electronic ambient — the sound of a datacenter.

### Beat 7 — The Return + Tagline (0:47 – 1:00)

**Action:** Cut back to ACHEEVY on the dock. The Spinner arcs back into frame from the sunset and snaps cleanly into its bandolier slot — a soft magnetic click. ACHEEVY's stance is unchanged. Commanding. Unbothered.

**Wide pull-back:** camera rises 10m, revealing the whole port. The containers all glow now. Boomer_Angs in silhouette at different stations, each equipped with their own Spinner — hip-holstered. A pair of wood storks cross the sky. The sun has lowered another degree — the light is now amber-bronze.

**Tagline lockup (on-screen, centered, Outfit typeface):**

> **FOAI**  
> *The Future of AI. Returned.*

Below it, smaller:

> The Deploy Platform. Per|Form. AIMS.
> Made In PLR (Pooler, GA) — wood stork mark.

**Sound:** the ambient bed resolves to a single warm chord. Hold. Fade.

## HeyGen v4 specific notes

- Use ONE canonical ACHEEVY reference video as the identity anchor. All scenes with ACHEEVY (Beats 1, 2, 7) render against the same reference so the character is continuous.
- Use a SECOND reference for the Per|Form athlete (Beat 5).
- The Lil_Hawks in Beat 6 are *non-HeyGen* — they're generated characters via Recraft/Ideogram + animated in post. HeyGen is for humanoid faces; Lil_Hawks are stylized birds.
- Spinner flights are **all VFX composite**, not HeyGen. HeyGen renders ACHEEVY throwing; the Spinner itself is animated as a separate layer in After Effects or Resolve Fusion.

## Negative constraints (must not appear)

- No firearms, no bladed weapons, no cyber-weapon visualizations — ever
- No face visible under ACHEEVY's visor
- No pelicans (only wood storks and herons)
- No occult / mystical / tarot imagery
- No dystopian grimdark lighting — golden hour, warm palette only
- No "AI thinking" brain visualizations — those are a design cliché
- No literal keyboards typing out text character-by-character — that's the GenAI cliché the Open Mind Skill exists to avoid

## Music direction

Track brief for a composer or library search:
- **Reference vibes:** *Blade Runner 2049* main theme restraint, *Arrival* opening sparsity, *The Social Network* credits pulse (slowed), *Oblivion (2013)* soundtrack warmth
- **Avoid:** trailer trap/dubstep, cinematic BRAAM hits, hip-hop drop (save for Per|Form-specific cuts elsewhere), orchestral crescendo cliché
- **Structure:** low drone + pad through Beats 1–2, add low pulse Beat 3, add melodic lead Beat 4, peak melodic sparsity Beat 5 (the key scene), ops-ambient under Beat 6, resolution chord Beat 7

## What this commercial proves (the Open-Mind check)

Pre-Mortem inversion: *how could this ad fail?* The failure modes I can see:

- Becomes another cinematic AI ad (there are already many) — **mitigated** by the Spinner mechanic (nobody else has a returning-boomerang execution verb) and by Beat 5's literal paperwork-and-performance duality.
- Feels militarized despite our armament rule — **mitigated** by the golden-hour warmth, by Spinner never being thrown AT anything (it's thrown THROUGH work), by the absence of target-silhouette framing.
- Feels generic sci-fi — **mitigated** by the coastal Georgia setting, the wood storks, the practical detail of NIL paperwork in Beat 5.
- Doesn't earn the names — **mitigated** by one scene per platform, each explicitly showing the literal thing the name claims.

Passes the Open Mind filter. Ship it.
