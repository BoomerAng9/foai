# Temecula Custom-Blend / Private-Label Service — Coastal Brewing Co.

**Researched**: 2026-04-29 (Sqwaadrun research lane)
**Owner directive**: "Lowcountry blend is ok, if we can order a mix from Temecula."
**Verdict**: **PARTIAL — yes private-label, no true custom-recipe blending.**

---

## Service available?

**PARTIAL.** Temecula Coffee Roasters DOES offer private-label and full-service drop-shipping. Customers can put their own brand/logo on Temecula's existing 46+ coffee offerings and 9 tea offerings. They DO NOT advertise from-scratch custom-recipe blending where Coastal would specify "this much Colombia + this much Sumatra + this much Ethiopia, roast medium" as a brand-new recipe. The "blends" sold by Temecula are Temecula's own house blends (6-Bean Espresso, African Espresso, Blonde Blend, Breakfast Blend, Cowboy Blend, etc.) — not customer-designed.

What Coastal CAN do today:

- Pick any of Temecula's 46+ coffees (single-origin or one of their pre-existing blends)
- Brand it as "Lowcountry House Blend" with Coastal label, bag design, cupping notes
- Ship under Coastal's name with Coastal branding
- Carry Temecula's certifications on the label (Fairtrade where applicable, Swiss Water for the Peru decaf, Trees for Education, World Coffee Research)

What Coastal CANNOT do today (without contacting Temecula directly):

- Specify a from-scratch origin mix recipe ("60% Colombia + 30% Sumatra + 10% Honduras")
- Get a custom roast profile dialed in for our brand voice
- Confirm that custom-recipe blending is even possible — page does not advertise it

**Recommendation**: Treat Temecula's private-label as "rebrand existing SKU" not "design new blend." If Coastal wants a true custom recipe, it is a sales-rep conversation, not a self-serve flow.

## Custom-blend process (as documented)

Per the private-label page, the process is a **4-step rebrand flow**, NOT a custom-recipe flow:

1. Upload your logo and bag design (Coastal provides design, or pay Temecula's design service)
2. Choose a coffee from Temecula's catalog (existing roast profile + cupping notes pre-defined)
3. Temecula handles roasting, packaging, shipping under Coastal's brand
4. Coastal pays per unit sold

To get a true custom recipe ("design a Lowcountry blend from scratch"), the path is:

- Email **coffee@temeculacoffeeroasters.com**
- Or attend the **Thursday Zoom Q&A** for prospective partners (advertised on the FAQ)
- Or visit the facility: 43064 Blackdeer Loop Suite F, Temecula, CA 92590

Temecula does NOT publicly advertise this as a self-serve product, so the actual capability + pricing must be confirmed by Coastal directly.

## Cert pass-through

**YES — for Fairtrade, Swiss Water, World Coffee Research, Trees for Education.** The private-label page states verbatim:

> "Direct partnerships exist with Fairtrade, World Coffee Research, Swiss Water®, and Trees for Education, allowing partners to participate in these programs to add credibility to their brands and add logos to packaging."

So a Coastal "Lowcountry Single-Origin Peru" SKU rebranded from TCR-FT-PERU-12OZ would carry Fairtrade on the Coastal bag.

**ORGANIC IS NOT YET AVAILABLE.** Confirmed 2026-04-29: Temecula's customer-service note says "we are working on the organic label." So Coastal cannot put a USDA Organic seal on a Coastal-branded Temecula coffee today. If Organic on the bag is non-negotiable, this is a wait-or-pivot decision.

For tea: ingredient-level organic claims (e.g., "Organic Assam TGFOP") can pass through to Coastal's bag as an ingredient list, but the FINISHED PRODUCT cannot carry a USDA Organic seal unless Temecula's tea facility is whole-product certified — which is not currently advertised.

## Recommendation: Lowcountry House Blend

**RECOMMEND PROCEED — but as a rebranded single-origin, not a true custom recipe — UNTIL the sales conversation confirms otherwise.**

Map "Lowcountry House Blend" to Temecula's existing **Fairtrade Peru — Medium Roast** (TCR-FT-PERU-12OZ) for these reasons:

- It is the SOLE Temecula SKU in the Clean Coffee Project (Eurofins lab-tested glyphosate-free + heavy-metals + mycotoxin clean) — strongest brand-asset story on the line
- Smooth medium roast with "lemon, herbal, milk chocolate" notes — fits the existing Coastal voice-library description "smooth medium-roast house blend"
- Carries Fairtrade cert which can pass through to the Coastal bag
- Single-cert (Fairtrade) qualifies under owner's relaxed filter

If owner wants a TRUE custom blend with a multi-origin recipe, the proposed origin mix for Lowcountry character (Lowcountry = Charleston / coastal Carolina, salt-air mornings, slow-porch evenings) would be:

- 50% Fairtrade Honduras Copan (Medium/Dark — cocoa, brown sugar, smooth body — the porch-evening backbone)
- 30% Fairtrade Colombia Tolima (Medium — caramel, citrus brightness — the morning-wake top note)
- 20% Fairtrade Peru Amazonas (Medium — lemon, herbal — the bridge between)

This three-origin mix gives a balanced cup that drinks well across the day and tells a Coastal story — three Fairtrade cooperatives, three character lanes, one Lowcountry blend. Temecula's sales rep would need to confirm this is buildable.

## Fallback if Temecula custom-recipe blending is NOT available

Three alternatives, ranked:

1. **KEEP TEMECULA, MAP TO PERU FAIRTRADE** — the Clean Coffee Project lab-tested asset is unique and the simplest path. Coastal's "Lowcountry House Blend" becomes a single-origin rebrand of Fairtrade Peru with Coastal cupping notes. Lowest effort, fastest ship, single-cert qualifies.

2. **COOPERATIVE COFFEES (Atlanta, GA)** — Fair-trade-only roaster cooperative, member-owned. Multi-origin blends with Fairtrade + many Organic SKUs available. Strong Lowcountry geography fit (Atlanta is regional). Higher onboarding friction (must qualify as a member or partner).

3. **EQUATOR COFFEES (San Rafael, CA)** — verified Fair Trade + USDA Organic + Regenerative Organic Certified across multiple SKUs. Their French Roast FT-Organic 12oz at $19.75 is the cleanest dual-cert reference price in the comp set. Custom-blend program advertised. Higher wholesale cost than Temecula but stronger cert pass-through (the only true Organic-certified product Coastal would have access to in this lane).
