## 2. Highest-Probability Fits (Coastal at current scale, Pooler GA)

1. **Food Lion Local Goodness** — Coastal sits inside their 10-state Southeast footprint. Local Goodness team does in-person visits. Coffee is in-scope (sauces/snacks/drinks named explicitly). Lowest barrier, fastest contract.
2. **Publix (regional Florida/Georgia + GreenWise Market POURS bar)** — Kahwa Coffee (Tampa) is the proven precedent: a regional roaster whose bagged beans went on Publix shelves AND whose brewed coffee is served in GreenWise cafes. Coastal can pitch the same dual play (bagged retail + GreenWise POURS pour-over). Pooler GA puts Coastal in Publix's home region.

Walmart Open Call and Sam's Club are premature — both need national co-packer infrastructure Coastal doesn't have. Kroger has no GA banner footprint.

## 3. Wholesale Price Floor (Back-of-Envelope)

Target grocery shelf: $13.99–$19.99 (12oz, below current $24.99 Tier-A DTC anchor).

- Retailer gross margin requirement: 35–40% (Publix/Food Lion class)
- At $15.99 retail × (1 − 0.38) = **$9.91 wholesale per 12oz bag**
- At $19.99 retail × (1 − 0.38) = **$12.39 wholesale per 12oz bag**
- Coastal current cost-to-produce floor (per pricing canon): cost + $1.50 minimum

If COGS per 12oz bag is ~$6.50 (green + roast + bag + label), wholesale floor of **~$9.50–10.00** preserves $3.00–3.50 contribution margin per bag — workable on volume, painful on small-batch single-origins. **Tier A blends only** for grocery; reserve rare/single-origins for DTC.

## 4. DTC vs. Grocery Channel-Conflict

- Retailers do **not** legally restrict DTC pricing, but they enforce **MAP (Minimum Advertised Price)** and **price-parity expectations** — your DTC bag cannot be cheaper than their shelf price after subscription discount.
- Grocery presence **dilutes** the membership pitch on commodity SKUs but **strengthens** brand awareness funnel into DTC for premium SKUs.
- **Precedent:** La Colombe + Whole Foods (cafes + wholesale + DTC subscription coexist). Stumptown runs DTC subscription + JAB-leveraged grocery RTD. Both keep **single-origin / limited-release / subscription-exclusive** lines off retail shelves to protect membership value.
- **Recommendation for Coastal:** Three-tier SKU partition — (a) Grocery shelf SKUs ($15.99–$19.99 blends), (b) DTC standard SKUs ($24.99 Tier A anchor), (c) Membership-exclusive single-origins / Habbak / micro-lot (no retail).

## 5. Owner's First Action

**Apply to Food Lion Local Goodness via RangeMe.** Single-step onboarding to all 5 retailers' discovery pipelines.

**Pre-application checklist:**
- UPC / GTIN-12 codes (one per SKU) — ~$250 via GS1 US
- Nutrition Facts panel (FDA-compliant) — coffee is exempt from full NLEA but needs allergen/origin/weight
- Product liability insurance — $1M/$2M general liability minimum (Food Lion requires)
- Co-packer / commercial-kitchen letter (Pooler facility documentation)
- W-9, EIN, certificate of insurance
- Sell sheet: 1-page with product photo, UPC, case pack (typical 6×12oz), wholesale price, MSRP, lead time
- RangeMe profile with all of the above + brand story (agentic experience layer is differentiation here)
