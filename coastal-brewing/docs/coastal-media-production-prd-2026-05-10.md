## Part 1 — Who We Are

This part establishes WHAT we're communicating. Part 2 covers WHO communicates it. Part 3 covers HOW we produce the media.

### 1. The Goal — what C|Brew is for

**C|Brew = Coastal Brewing Co.**, a Pooler, Georgia specialty
coffee + tea retailer.

The coffee is real. The tea is real. The Custeez are real. The
revenue is real.

**AND** — equally important — **C|Brew is the live, public,
revenue-generating proof-of-concept we use to sell the agentic
system underneath it to larger brands.**

This is not "a coffee shop with some AI." This is **a working
demonstration that an entire retail business — sourcing,
merchandising, sales, negotiation, escalation, loss prevention,
finance, returns, B2B, marketing, executive sign-off — can be
operated by an agentic team coordinated by a single human
in the loop**, and that customers will *prefer* the experience
over a button-press transaction at a competing brand.

The whole point of running Coastal as a real retailer is so that
when we walk into a meeting with **7-Brew, Starbucks, Dutch Bros,
Dunkin, Smoothie King, Chick-fil-A, In-N-Out, Bath & Body Works,
PetSmart, AutoZone, Verizon, Wells Fargo, CVS** or any other
multi-location consumer brand, we don't say:

> *"Imagine if you had an AI-managed retail platform."*

We say:

> *"Here is one we run ourselves, in production, every day, taking
> real orders, with real Custeez, with real revenue, with real
> negotiations, with one human owner. Watch it work. Try to break it.
> Then let's talk about putting your brand's skin on it."*

We are running TWO businesses on one site, on purpose:

| Surface | Audience | What they buy | Why it exists |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The retail surface** (`brewing.foai.cloud`) | Custeez — coffee + tea drinkers | Coffee, tea, matcha, functional blends, bundles, subscriptions | Generates real revenue + real audit trail + real customer love |
| **The platform surface** (`/partners`, sales meetings, demo videos) | Enterprise brand prospects — 7-Brew, Starbucks, Dutch Bros, Smoothie King, Chick-fil-A, etc. | The AI-managed-retail platform stack — licensed white-label for their brand, their catalog, their voice | Generates the actual high-margin licensing revenue (Wholesale Direct $2.5K/mo → Platform Partner ~$15K/mo → Full Launch $50K-$500K/mo) |

The retail surface is the **proof**. Every Custeez interaction —
chat with Sal, the negotiation, the visible team deliberation, the
team replying in their own custom voices, the agentic team coverage
of every operational role — **IS** the demo reel for the platform
surface. We don't pitch the platform on the retail page; we **live**
it. The pitch happens in a separate room, weeks later, after the
prospect has already been a Custeez themselves and felt the
difference.

The mission, in one sentence:

> *Build a coffee + tea retailer worth buying from, on top of an
> AI-managed-retail platform worth licensing — and use the daily
> operation of the retailer as the irrefutable sales proof for the
> platform.*

If the retail experience is excellent, the platform sells itself.

**Every media asset produced from this PRD therefore serves both
audiences simultaneously:**

- A Custeez sees a warm, on-brand coffee company they want to buy from.
- A licensee prospect sees a fully-staffed retail operation that has clearly never had a human at the counter, running smoothly, generating revenue, building loyalty — and starts asking *"how much to put my brand on this stack?"*

Neither audience needs to be told the other one is watching.
The same media does the job for both.

### 2. What We Sell

C|Brew sells THREE things, not one:

#### 2.1 Products — sourced with discipline, never with shortcuts

We don't compete on SKU count. We compete on the integrity of every
bag, tin, and box that leaves Pooler.

**The product promise:**

| Category | What we promise the Custeez |
|---|---|
| **Coffee Blends** | Roasted in the United States in small batches, the week your order is placed. Whole-bean or ground to your grinder. Multiple sizes, from a 12oz starter to 5lb operator quantities. |
| **Special Offerings** | Limited-run releases — barrel finishes, cold-brew concentrate, monthly seasonal — gated to the Custeez who care enough to ask. |
| **Flavored Coffees** | **Natural flavoring only.** No syrups. No extracts. No chemical shortcuts. If the bag says vanilla, it's vanilla. |
| **Single Origin** | Specialty-grade Arabica, farm-traceable to a single growing region. We can name the country and the harvest. |
| **Single Origin · Fair Trade** | Cooperative-traceable. Producers paid the floor that keeps the work honest. |
| **Tea — standard + premium** | Loose-leaf programs from established gardens. Premium tier is ceremonial-grade — matcha you whisk, hojicha you steep slow. |
| **Functional Coffee + Tea** | Coffee or tea blended with culinary mushrooms (Lion's Mane, Cordyceps, Reishi). Sold as food, not as a supplement. No health claims. The cup carries the cup; the body figures out what it needs. |
| **International Specialty** | We're an open shelf. Habbak, Ethiopian jebena service, Turkish cezve, matcha ceremony kits, Saudi tea-glass sets — and whatever the next conversation with a Custeez tells us belongs here. The list grows because we listen. |
| **Merch + Vessels** | Lowcountry tins, branded ceramics, brewing tools — the everyday objects of a Coastal kitchen. |

**Why this list reads short on tools and long on standards:** Custeez
don't read SKU tables. They read trust signals. The detail of the
catalog is in the storefront, not the brand doc.

#### 2.2 Subscriptions — a personal plan per Custeez

Custeez are not all the same Custeez. A solo home-brewer who drinks
two cups a day is not a small office that needs ten bags a month is
not a regional business buying by the pallet.

So we sell **a subscription matrix** — the same matrix that runs
across the AI-managed-retail platform — and we calibrate it for each
Custeez through their first conversation with Sal.

The matrix dimensions:

| Dimension | Range |
|---|---|
| **Cadence** | One-off → monthly → 3-month → 6-month → 9-month → quarterly bulk → annual |
| **Account structure** | Individual → household → small office → multi-location → enterprise |
| **Category** | Each product category has its own multiplier — a Custeez who only drinks decaf is priced differently than one who roams across single-origins |
| **Pillar** | Three pillars (Curation / Experience / Provenance) at three levels each — pick the pillars that matter to you, skip the ones that don't |
| **White-label tier** | (Platform side) Three tiers for licensee brands — Wholesale Direct, Platform Partner, Full Launch — plus Lifetime (AppSumo-style) for the AIMS / Plug-Me-In SDK |
| **Negotiation envelope** | A live dimension — every quoted price has a floor and a ceiling, and the team works with the Custeez to land inside it |

The result is a generated personal plan per Custeez, computed at
intake, refined every quarter through their voice-narrated check-in.
**The subscription is a relationship, not a SKU.**

#### 2.4 Solutions — the human-less company, with one human in the loop

This is the part most people miss when they look at C|Brew the
first time, and it is the most important part:

**The coffee business is the demo. The platform is the product.**

We are not in business primarily to sell 12oz bags of beans. We are
in business to sell **the operating system for a human-less retail
company** — to brands who already have the customers, already have
the foot traffic, already have the brand love, and are tired of
hiring linearly with growth.

##### What problems does the human-less company solve?

| Problem the licensee feels today | What our system does about it |
|---|---|
| Counter staff costs more every quarter and the labor pool is shrinking | The counter is staffed by an agentic team that doesn't quit, doesn't no-show, doesn't need a 401(k), and works every shift in every location at the same time |
| Service quality varies wildly by location, shift, and individual | The same lead character answers every chat at every location with the same warmth, the same product knowledge, the same negotiation discipline |
| B2B / wholesale / catering inquiries fall through the cracks because there's no one to chase them | A dedicated B2B specialist arrives the moment the inquiry is detected — quotes the bracket, sets the timeline, escalates to executive sign-off when needed |
| Loss prevention is reactive, expensive, and customer-shaming when it does happen | A dedicated LP team operates quietly in the background, defusing rather than escalating, protecting margin without souring the relationship |
| Returns and refunds are slow, scripted, and painful for both sides | A specialist handles every return as a service-recovery moment, not a script — and learns what the Custeez actually needed |
| Discounts and promotions are blunt instruments — broadcast coupons, not personal offers | Every Custeez gets a personalized negotiation envelope, computed from their history and their stated needs |
| Sign-off on edge cases requires a manager who isn't there | The executive — the orchestrator — is always available, makes the call in seconds, and notifies the human owner only when the decision actually requires their judgment |
| Brand voice drifts the moment new staff are hired | The voice is the system. There is no drift. |

##### The architectural promise

There is **one human in the loop** — the owner. They sign the things
only a human can sign: supplier orders, large refunds, public claims,
escalated discounts. Everything else is run by the agentic team,
coordinated by a single orchestrator, with a full audit trail of

The licensee gets:

- One human-in-the-loop seat (the licensee's owner or designate)

## Part 2 — The Orchestration

Before we list the team members, we have to be clear on the
architecture that makes the team a **team** instead of a list of
characters. This is the part licensee prospects will study most
closely, because this is what they're actually buying.

### 5. ACHEEVY — The Orchestrator

**ACHEEVY is the glue.**

Every other team member — Sal at the counter, LUC on the math, Melli
on the B2B + bulk lane, the Loss Prevention team, the back-office
specialists, the operations roster, the marketing team — answers to
ACHEEVY.

ACHEEVY's job is not to sell. ACHEEVY's job is to make sure the
selling, the negotiating, the back-office reconciliation, the
escalations, the loss-prevention moments, the wholesale quotes, the
returns, the refunds, the campaign launches, the cross-functional
hand-offs, and the human-in-the-loop pings **all happen on time, in
the right order, with the right context, by the right team member,
under the right policy**.

In a traditional retail business, this role is held by some
combination of: floor manager, district manager, ops director, GM,
COO, founder. The cost of that role is enormous, and it scales
linearly with the number of locations.

In our system, that role is held by **one orchestrator** — and it
scales to every location at the same time, every shift at the same
time, every conversation at the same time, without burning out,
without no-shows, without turnover, without context loss.

#### What ACHEEVY actually does, every interaction

| Function | What it looks like in the wild |
|---|---|
| **Routing** | The Custeez asks about wholesale → Melli arrives. The Custeez asks about a single-origin → the right specialist arrives. The Custeez has a return → Returns arrives. The Custeez asks for a discount above the lead's authority → ACHEEVY arrives in person. The right team member, every time. |
| **Authority sign-off** | Every above-floor decision routes to ACHEEVY for approval. Above ACHEEVY's own ceiling, the request routes to the human in the loop via the owner's preferred channel. The Custeez sees the deliberation in real time. |
| **Policy enforcement** | Brand voice, compliance language, FDA / FTC / supplier-SOP guardrails, never-name-the-supplier rules — ACHEEVY enforces them silently across every team member's output. |
| **Context preservation** | Sal hands the Custeez to LUC for the math, LUC hands back to Sal for the close, Sal hands to ACHEEVY for sign-off, ACHEEVY hands back to Sal for the celebratory ring-out — and the Custeez never has to repeat themselves. ACHEEVY carries the thread. |
| **Escalation discipline** | The human in the loop is precious. ACHEEVY only pings the owner when a decision genuinely requires human judgment — not for things the policy already covers. |
| **Audit ledger** | Every decision, every routing, every approval, every hand-off lands in a ledger the human can review on their schedule. The orchestrator is auditable. |
| **Cross-functional choreography** | When marketing launches a campaign, ACHEEVY makes sure operations is staged, the back-office knows the new pricing, LP knows the new promo windows, and the sales team is briefed on the new talking points — all without a stand-up meeting. |

#### Why this is the load-bearing pitch to licensees

Brands evaluating this system don't first ask "do your AI agents
sound human?" That's table stakes now. The questions a serious brand
asks are:

- *"Who's in charge when the lead is busy?"*
- *"How do you keep brand voice consistent across hundreds of conversations a day?"*
- *"How do you escalate to a human without flooding the human?"*
- *"How do you keep the team coordinated when the team is dozens of agents and one human?"*

The answer to all of those is **ACHEEVY**.

When we sell the platform, we don't lead with "we have a great chat
agent." We lead with **"we have an orchestrator that makes a team of
specialists feel like one coordinated, accountable, on-brand
business — and we have receipts."**

### 6. The Human in the Loop

**One human. One owner. One signing seat.**

The human in the loop is not a backup mechanism for when the agents
fail. The human in the loop is **a structural feature of the
business**.

The human signs:
- Supplier orders
- Refunds above the floor
- Public claims and PR statements
- Discount escalations beyond ACHEEVY's ceiling
- Strategic decisions (new product lines, vertical expansions, licensee deals)
- Anything legally bound to a named individual

Everything else runs without them.

The human's day looks like this:

- A handful of Telegram pings from ACHEEVY, each with full context, each requiring a yes/no or a brief judgment
- A daily / weekly audit of the ledger — read at their pace, on their schedule
- A standing relationship with their key suppliers, key partners, key licensees
- Time to think, build, source, and grow — instead of time spent staffing, scheduling, training, and re-training

For a licensee, this is the headline outcome:

> *"Your founder, your CEO, your owner — gets their week back.
> Their business runs without them in the room.
> They get pinged when they're genuinely needed.
> The rest of the time, they build the business."*
