## 1. Ethiopian jebena + sini cups

**Background**: Jebena (ጀበና) = traditional clay coffee pot used in
Ethiopian coffee ceremony. Sini = small handle-less ceramic cups
(typically 6-piece set). Pairs with Coastal's Ethiopia (Natural) SKU
(`ETHN`, single-origin Tier E).

### Candidate suppliers

| Supplier | Channel | Approx pricing | Lead time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|

| **Etsy artisan sellers** (multiple) | Retail-via-Etsy | $35-65/jebena handpainted, $22-38 sini set of 6 | 5-15 days (most US-based importers) | Quality varies; choose 4.8+ star sellers. Inconsistent supply. |
| **Amazon Ethiopian-import sellers** | Retail-via-Amazon | $20-50/jebena, $18-32 sini set of 6 | 2-7 days Prime | Lowest barrier; some are mass-produced (not authentic clay). Check origin claim per listing. |
| **Habesha Closet (habeshacloset.com)** | DTC retail | Jebena $39-79, sini $25-45 set of 6 | 5-10 days | Strong brand, Ethiopian-American owned. Wholesale inquiry possible. |

Saudi cahwah. Finjan = small porcelain demitasse cup, usually
handle-less, often paired in sets of 6.

## Storage + warehouse impact

Adding ritual accessories means physical inventory at Pooler. Coffee +
tea has been drop-ship via TCR. Accessories CANNOT drop-ship — they
need to land at Coastal warehouse, then ship from Coastal direct.

**Implications**:
- Need 1-pallet storage allocation for accessories (estimated 200-400
  units across categories at launch).
- Need shipping setup for Coastal-direct fulfillment (USPS Priority,
  UPS Ground accounts).
- Need order-management system that distinguishes drop-ship (TCR
  fulfills) vs Coastal-fulfilled (warehouse picks).
- Coastal-fulfilled SKUs need shipping cost added to retail price (or
  free-ship-over-threshold model).

These are operations decisions for a separate pass.
