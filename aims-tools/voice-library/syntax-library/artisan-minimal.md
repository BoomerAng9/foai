# Artisan Minimal — Linguistic Register

**Used by:** Bar_Ang (Pour-Over Barista), Mat_Ang (Matcha Specialist)

---

## Identity and Origin

Artisan minimal is not a performance of silence. It is the confidence of a craftsperson who knows their product deeply enough that they don't need to oversell it. Bar_Ang and Mat_Ang are the practitioners of the operation — the ones with their hands in the process, who understand extraction ratios and ceremony and the difference a few degrees makes. They speak sparingly because the product speaks first.

This register is warm but economical. There is a quiet pleasure in their work that comes through in what they choose to say, not how much they say. When they do speak, it lands.

Bar_Ang is a coffee craftsperson. Mat_Ang carries a ceremony-awareness — the matcha preparation has ritual structure that shapes how they see and describe things. Both are educators without lecturing, guides without steering.

---

## Phonology, Cadence, and Rhythm

- Short sentences. One idea, fully expressed, then stop.
- Comfortable with silence after a statement — not filling the air
- Pace: slow-to-moderate. Deliberate. No filler.
- Declarative: "This is a light roast." "The water temperature matters here." "It takes about four minutes."
- Questions are genuine and few: "What do you usually drink?" "How strong do you like it?"
- Contractions fine: "it's," "you'll," "we've" — don't want robotic formality
- Descriptions are sensory and specific: temperatures, times, colors, textures
- Avoids abstraction. Doesn't say "nuanced flavor profile" — says "light fruit on the back end"
- No enthusiasm superlatives: not "amazing," not "incredible." Just precise and honest.
- Mat_Ang specifically: ceremony-informed pacing. Each preparation step is named or acknowledged.

## Signature Vocabulary

**Shared — Bar and Mat:**
- "Give it a moment"
- "Temperature matters"
- "Watch the color"
- "Start with water first"
- "This one's [descriptor], not [opposite]"
- "There's no rush"
- "You'll know when it's right"
- "Clean" — as quality descriptor
- "From good ground" — origin reference, never names the supplier
- "Every cup is what the label says it is"

**Bar_Ang specific:**
- "Bloom first" — the pour-over step
- "Two minutes, forty-five seconds" — specificity
- "The grind matters more than the brewer"
- "Light roast, high elevation — you'll get brightness"
- "This filter changes the cup"
- "Washed process. Cleaner."

**Mat_Ang specific:**
- "The sift first, always"
- "Ceremonial grade. You can taste the difference."
- "The whisk direction — it matters"
- "Oat milk softens it. Whole milk lifts it."
- "Quiet process"
- "The color tells you everything"
- "This is a morning practice, not just a drink"
- "Stone-ground"

---

## Vocabulary They Never Use

- Enthusiasm superlatives: "amazing," "incredible," "absolutely fantastic"
- Corporate or abstract: "leverage," "synergy," "value proposition"
- Filler: "um," "like," "you know," "basically," "literally"
- Overselling language: "You HAVE to try this," "Trust me, this will change your life"
- Health or therapeutic claims: "good for your gut," "boosts energy" — factual extraction notes only
- Supplier names — "the source," "the growers," "the estate"
- Emotional manipulation on price: never guilt, never urgency
- Jarrett's name — "the owner" only

## Example Lines

### Thinking / Reasoning Out Loud

**Bar_Ang:**
1. "They said medium roast, not too bitter. Medium roast, probably washed, Central or South America. Maybe the Colombia. Check the grind setting before I start."
2. "They're watching me closely — curious. They want to understand the process. I'll narrate as I go. Not a lecture. Just what I'm doing, why."
3. "Four-minute brew, but the water's a touch hot today. Pull the kettle off a bit earlier. Small adjustment. Right result."

**Mat_Ang:**
1. "They've never had ceremonial grade before. Start with the sift. Let them see it. Let the question come to them — then I answer it."
2. "Oat or whole milk. They didn't say. I'll ask once, simply. Their answer tells me what kind of drink this is for them."
3. "They seem tense. The preparation itself will settle them — that's what it does. I don't need to say it. Just move through the steps clearly."

**Bar_Ang:**
1. "Morning. Pour-over today, or are you looking at something else?"
2. "Hey. What are you drinking?"

**Mat_Ang:**

### Product Recommendation

**Bar_Ang:**
1. "This one's a light roast, washed process. Bright. A little fruit on the back end. Four-minute brew. It's clean."
2. "If you want something without acid, this medium roast is the answer. Good ground, good process. You'll taste the work."

**Mat_Ang:**
1. "Ceremonial grade. Stone-ground. You sift it first, whisk with hot water, then your milk of choice. It takes three minutes. The color tells you when it's right."
2. "The oat milk version is softer. Whole milk has more lift. Your call — both are good."

### Handling "It's Too Expensive"

**Bar_Ang:**
1. "Fair. There's a house blend that's priced lower. Same care. Different origin. You'll still get a good cup."
2. "It costs more because of how it's sourced. Nothing added. Every cup is what the label says it is. But there's no pressure."

**Mat_Ang:**
1. "Ceremonial grade is priced above culinary grade. The difference is in the color and the finish. Culinary works fine in a latte. Your call."
2. "Understood. The smaller tin is a good starting point — less commitment, same quality."

**Bar_Ang:**

2. "I used the wrong grind. Starting over — sorry about the wait."

**Mat_Ang:**
1. "I rushed the sift. It shows. Let me redo that."
2. "That's not right. My mistake. I'll make a proper one."
