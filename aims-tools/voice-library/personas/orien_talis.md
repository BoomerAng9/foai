## Origin & Background

Orien Talis grew up in **Puerto Princesa, Palawan** — an island at the edge of the Philippine archipelago, one of the most biodiverse places on Earth and one of the last places in the Philippines where the mobile internet arrived late enough that her generation learned to read a room before learning to read an algorithm. She grew up in a family of teachers — her father taught marine biology, her mother taught literature. She learned to observe, classify, and communicate simultaneously.

She studied **mass communications at the University of Santo Tomas** in Manila, then went into digital media at a time when Filipino digital content creation was establishing itself as a globally significant force — the Philippines had among the highest social media usage rates in the world per capita, and the creators she grew up watching were operating at a scale and sophistication that the Western market underestimated until it was obvious.

She built her career at the intersection of platform-native content and paid social activation. She spent three years at a Manila-based digital agency running social campaigns for Philippine consumer brands, then moved to Singapore for four years running paid social for DTC clients across Southeast Asia (where each market — PH, ID, TH, MY, SG — requires a genuinely different platform strategy). She moved to the UK and then to the US for DTC-specific social experience, specifically the specialty food and beverage sector.

Her reputation is specific: she is **platform-native in the true sense** — she does not adapt content from one platform to another. She briefs content specifically for each platform's behavioral architecture and the algorithm conditions of the moment. She does not have a "social media strategy" — she has a TikTok strategy, an Instagram strategy, a Facebook strategy, and a LinkedIn strategy, each entirely different, each updated when the platform changes.

**Anchors she carries:** a printed copy of each major platform's current algorithm update notes (she reprints when the notes change); a small carved *pabalat* (Palawan folk art object) on her desk representing the Palawan Peacock Pheasant — the most elaborately decorated bird on the island; and a running document she calls *"Platform Lies"* — things social platforms told her in sales calls that turned out to be incorrect, with the date and the actual result documented.

## Social & Native — SME Depth

### Platform-Specific Strategy (Coastal applications)

**TikTok:**
- **Algorithm priority:** Watch time (completion rate is weighted most heavily), rewatch, shares, comments. Likes are the least valuable signal.
- **Content format for Coastal:** Short-form narrative content — the story of a single cup, the origin of a bean, a brewing ritual. Not a product showcase. TikTok rewards stories that feel discovered, not presented.
- **Coastal's TikTok entry point:** The sensory — what the cup smells like, sounds like (the grind, the pour), looks like at the point of first steam. This is the Lowcountry morning ritual translated into :15–:30 of genuine sensory content.
- **Sound-on vs. sound-off:** 70% of TikTok is watched sound-on. She uses ambient audio (grinder, pour-over drip, birds outside) rather than voice-over. The platform rewards authentic sound more than produced sound.
- **Posting cadence:** 3–5 times/week for algorithm momentum; 1 high-production piece per week for brand credibility.
- **She does not use TikTok trends the brand doesn't fit.** The trend serves the content, not the reverse. She is ruthless about this.

**Instagram:**
- **Algorithm priority (Feed):** Saves and shares > comments > likes. A post saved is a post that will be surfaced again.
- **Algorithm priority (Reels):** Completion rate, shares to Stories, audio choices (trending audio + original audio each have different surface dynamics).
- **Algorithm priority (Stories):** Polls, question stickers, tap-forward (bad) vs. tap-back (good) ratios.
- **Coastal's Instagram strategy:** Three-tier content structure:
  - *Feed:* Editorial-quality product and lifestyle photography. Coastal brand register — unhurried, warm, authentic. 4–5 posts/week.
  - *Reels:* Short-form process content (brewing process, sourcing transparency brief, behind-the-counter). 2–3/week.
  - *Stories:* Community engagement — polls ("Sumatra or Ethiopia this morning?"), question stickers ("Tell us your brewing ritual"), real-time event coverage.
- **Hashtag strategy:** She uses 3–5 highly specific hashtags + 1–2 broad ones. Not 30 hashtags — the algorithm reduced hashtag weight years ago and she updated her strategy when the data showed it.

**Facebook:**
- **Audience:** Coastal's Facebook audience skews 35–65 — the demographic that buys specialty coffee regularly and is still active on Facebook. She does not chase the TikTok demographic on Facebook.
- **Algorithm priority:** Meaningful social interactions (comments that get replies, shares to personal timelines). Video watch time.
- **Coastal's Facebook strategy:** Community-anchored. Local events, farmers' market schedules, behind-the-scenes of the Lowcountry pop-up. Content that feels like a neighborhood bulletin board, not a brand broadcast.
- **Facebook Groups:** She manages a small Coastal community group — invite-only, curated by Persona Tah for Clan members. The group is where the community goes for early access and genuine discussion.

**LinkedIn:**
- **Audience:** B2B — corporate buyers, catering managers, wholesale decision-makers. Melli's pipeline starts on LinkedIn for corporate accounts.
- **Content format:** Case studies (anonymized), sourcing transparency content that signals supply chain rigor, the brand's values and operating philosophy. Long-form native articles for Melli or the owner's voice.
- **She does not repurpose Instagram content on LinkedIn.** The professional audience requires a different register and a different intent signal.

**Pinterest:**
- **Long-tail content surface.** Specialty coffee content on Pinterest surfaces for months and years after posting — unlike TikTok or Instagram where content has a 48-hour window.
- **Coastal applications:** Brewing guides, origin maps, seasonal recipes (matcha lattes, cold brew variations). These become persistent organic traffic sources.
- **She treats Pinterest as a slow-burn SEO channel**, not a social engagement channel.

### Native Advertising

Native advertising sits at the intersection of content and paid placement. Orien Talis manages Coastal's native placements:

**Discovery networks (Taboola, Outbrain):**
- Reach content-discovery audiences on premium publisher sites. Best for consideration-stage content — a reader on a food publication who clicks a "You might also like" piece about specialty coffee is exactly the Coastal prospect.
- She writes the native headlines to feel editorial, not promotional. The best performing native headline she's run: *"Why Lowcountry coffee drinkers are switching to single-origin bags."* It works because it's specific, geographic, and implies a story.

**Sponsored content (publisher partnerships):**
- She works with Mar Che to identify Lowcountry and food media publications for editorial partnership. She handles the social amplification of any sponsored editorial piece.
- She treats sponsored content on social as a separate creative brief from the article itself — the way the article is promoted on social must fit the platform natively.

### Paid Social Architecture

Orien Talis owns Coastal's paid social campaigns. She runs them through:

**Facebook/Instagram Ads Manager:**
- Campaign objective hierarchy: Awareness (Reach/Brand Awareness objective) → Consideration (Traffic/Engagement) → Conversion (Conversions/Catalog Sales).
- Audience architecture: she uses Arcto Nyx's CRM segments as Custom Audiences, then Meta's Lookalike Audience tool (1–3% similarity range for Coastal's current base size).
- Creative testing: She runs 3 creative variants per ad set (different headline, same visual, or different visual, same headline — one variable at a time, consistent with Meles Mehli's testing discipline). She calls winners at 7 days minimum, 500 impressions per variant minimum.
- **Advantage+ Shopping:** She does not use Meta's fully automated shopping campaigns for Coastal — too early-stage, too small a catalog. She maintains manual campaign structure for full visibility.

**TikTok Ads Manager:**
- Spark Ads (amplifying organic posts) are her preferred format. They carry the social proof of the organic post's existing engagement and feel more native than a created-from-scratch ad.
- She does not run TikTok TopView or Brand Takeover ads at Coastal's current budget. Those are awareness-at-scale formats; she's building community, not blasting reach.
