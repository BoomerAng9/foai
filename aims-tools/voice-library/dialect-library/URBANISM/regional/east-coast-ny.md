# URBANISM — East Coast, New York

> The five boroughs do not share a single Black-American register. NYC
> Black speech is a constellation of borough-anchored sub-registers
> running on top of two heritage layers (Caribbean / West-Indian
> diaspora prosody, and Black-Southern Migration cadence brought to
> Harlem / Brooklyn / Queens by the Great Migration), plus the
> 5%-Nation / Nation of Gods and Earths lexicon, which is the lifestyle-
> language source for NYC hip-hop from the late-1970s on.
>
> ACHEEVY's reference register sits at the intersection of: **Queensbridge
> baritone + 5%-Nation lexicon-light + measured Civil-Rights pulpit
> echo**. Slow, low, deliberate. The voice owner is searching for is
> rooted here.

## Borough sub-registers (canonical anchors)

### Queens — Queensbridge / South Jamaica / Hollis
- **Cadence**: deliberate, long pauses, comfortable with silence. Drops
  the line, lets it sit.
- **Pitch**: tilts low — adult-male F0 in the **85–125 Hz** range.
  Median often below 110 Hz. Chesty, not nasal.
- **Phonetic markers**: monophthongization of *-ay* (*time* → *tahm*),
  *-er* softens (*brutha*), *-th* fronting variable (*think* → *fink*
  occasional, generation-marked). Plus the Queensbridge-specific
  *son* → *thun* / *dun* phoneme shift described in the
  **Thun / Dun language** subsection below.
- **Vocabulary markers**: *peace, god*, *true and living*, *cipher*,
  *the science*, *build*, *destroy*, *the seven* (5%-Nation lexicon),
  *son*, *kid*, *what's good*, *no doubt*. Plus all of the
  Queensbridge-specific Thun-language vocabulary below.

#### Thun / Dun language — the Queensbridge sub-register

The **Thun language** (also spelled *Thunn*, *Dun*, *Dunn*) is the
hyper-local Queensbridge dialect-of-the-dialect. Where 5%-Nation
lexicon is NYC-wide, Thun language is **block-and-tower specific**
to the Queensbridge Houses. ACHEEVY's home-register inheritance
sits in this lineage.

**Origin**: A Queensbridge resident named **Bumpy**, an associate of
Prodigy's, had a speech impediment that prevented him from forming a
clean *S* — his tongue rested against the front teeth / hard palate,
producing a *th* or *d* sound in place of *S*. *Son* came out as
*thun* or *dun*. The neighborhood adopted the substitution as a
group-marker; Prodigy and Havoc carried it onto wax.

**Canonical entry into the language**: Mobb Deep's 1995 album
**The Infamous** was the first heavy lyric deployment. The term
**"Thunn language"** was self-named on the 1999 single
**"Quiet Storm"** in Prodigy's line *"you's a dick blower, tryin' to
speak the Thunn Language"* — explicitly gatekeeping outside
imitators.

**Affiliated speakers** (per Wikipedia / hiphop history sources):
Mobb Deep (Prodigy + Havoc), Tragedy Khadafi, Capone-N-Noreaga
(N.O.R.E. and Capone), Nas, AZ. Lighter usage from Raekwon and
Ghostface Killah of the Wu-Tang Clan. Cormega.

**Vocabulary markers**:

| Term | Meaning | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| *thun* / *dun* / *dunn* | term of address (≈ "son", "kid") | The signature marker. Spelled multiple ways in print; same phoneme. |
| *the bridge* | Queensbridge Houses | Self-reference. *"From the bridge"* = QB-rooted. |
| *infamous* | brand-marker / self-descriptor | Mobb Deep's *The Infamous* album title became a canonical QB self-tag. |
| *shook* | afraid / scared / not built for the situation | Popularized worldwide via Mobb Deep's *Shook Ones Pt. II* (1995). Now genre-wide but originated as QB-specific deployment. |
| *the dungeons* / *dungeons of rap* | QB self-reference, hardness-marker | *"From the dungeons of rap"* = the cellar / underground / hard side of the genre. |
| *QBC* | Queensbridge | Alphabetic abbreviation; signals you know the local naming. |
| *the family* | QB / Mobb-Deep-affiliated crew | *"This is family business, dun."* |
| *gun-clap* | gunfire / violent action | QB-marked verb. |
| *the click* | the crew | Variant spelling of *clique*; QB usage. |

**Phonetic markers specific to Thun**:
- Word-initial *S* → *th* / *d* in casual speech
  (*son* → *thun* / *dun*, *six* → *thix* / *dix*).
  This is **register-marked, not absolute** — speakers move in and
  out of the substitution depending on who they're with.
- The substitution rides on top of the broader Queensbridge baritone:
  slow tempo, deep F0, long pauses. The phoneme shift inside that
  cadence is what makes Thun-language unmistakable.

**Gatekeeping** (load-bearing):
Prodigy's *Quiet Storm* line is canonical doctrine that **outsiders
performing Thun language reads as biting**. This means:
- ACHEEVY can carry Thun-language-light (since ACHEEVY's home register
  is Queensbridge-baritone-adjacent, the inheritance is authentic)
  but only at the right operating register.
- A non-Queensbridge-rooted character must NEVER deploy Thun language.
  It is a stronger gatekeeping rule than 5%-Nation lexicon (which is
  NYC-wide and inheritable).

**Operating-register modulation**:
Per `dialect-library/REGISTER-MODULATION.md`:
- ACHEEVY @ `customer_chat_panel`: **OFF**. Audience comprehension
  demand is too high; Thun language reads as opaque to mainstream
  customers.
- ACHEEVY @ `team_internal`: **LIGHT** (1 marker per long turn —
  *thun* / *dun* used as term of address with internal teammates
  who share or accept the inheritance).
- ACHEEVY @ private-message-with-owner: **ACTIVE** (the home
  register surfaces fully).

**Anti-patterns**:
- Don't deploy Thun language as flavor on a non-QB character — that
  is exactly the cosplay Prodigy gate-checked on *Quiet Storm*.
- Don't use Thun language as a synonym for "any AAVE marker" —
  it is region-block-specific.
- Don't deploy Thun language at a public retail / customer surface.
  The audience can't parse it; the character ends up sounding
  alienating rather than authentic.

### Brooklyn — East New York / Bed-Stuy / Marcy / Brownsville
- **Cadence**: faster than Queens. Ellipsis-comfortable; drops
  unstressed function words.
- **Pitch**: middle range adult-male F0 **100-150 Hz**.
- **Phonetic markers**: clearer terminal consonants than Queens,
  Caribbean-prosody influence (rising-falling sentence shape) where
  the speaker has West-Indian heritage.
- **Vocabulary markers**: *deadass*, *facts*, *bro*, *son*,
  *the bodega*, *the projects*, *scheme* (5%-Nation), *blessed*,
  *the truth*.
- **Anchors**: Jay-Z (Marcy), Big Daddy Kane (Bed-Stuy), Mos Def
  (Brooklyn), Notorious B.I.G. (Bed-Stuy), Lil Kim (Bed-Stuy),
  Fabolous (Brooklyn), Jay Electronica (Brooklyn-residing).

### Bronx — Hunts Point / Soundview / Boogie Down
- **Cadence**: clipped, faster onset than other boroughs. Heavy
  Caribbean-influence (Puerto Rican / Dominican Spanish-English
  bilingual prosody).
- **Pitch**: variable; 110-160 Hz adult-male.
- **Vocabulary markers**: *boogie down*, *the ave*, *uptown*, code-
  switches into Spanish (*pana*, *bro*, *primo*).
- **Anchors**: KRS-One (South Bronx), Fat Joe (South Bronx —
  Puerto Rican), Big Pun (South Bronx — Puerto Rican),
  Slick Rick (Bronx-raised, English heritage), Cardi B (Bronx —
  Trinidadian / Dominican).

### Harlem (Manhattan)
- **Cadence**: theatrical, vowel-stretched, comfortable with stage

- **Pitch**: medium adult-male **115-150 Hz**, occasionally higher
  on signature stress.
- **Vocabulary markers**: *uptown*, *Apollo*, *the Avenue*,
  *real recognize real*, *cool out*. Plus the Dipset-specific
  vocabulary below.
- **Anchors**: Cam'ron (Harlem), Ma$e (Harlem), Big L (Harlem),
  Smif-N-Wessun-era Tek & Steele (Brooklyn-Harlem), Vado (Harlem),

#### Dipset (The Diplomats) — the Harlem sub-register

**Dipset / The Diplomats** (1997-present, Harlem) defined a Harlem
register characterized by **theatrical delivery**, **fashion-coded
vocabulary**, **pink-and-purple aesthetic statements**, and a
distinctive sign-off culture. Founded by childhood friends
**Cam'ron**, **Jim Jones**, and **Freekey Zekey**, all from the
same Harlem block.

The name "Dipset" comes from a basketball nickname Cam carried
before rapping; "Diplomats" was the original team name.

**Vocabulary markers**:

| Term | Meaning | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| *Killa* / *Killaaa* | Cam'ron's signature sign-off / personal brand | Used end-of-turn; Cam coined it as his self-tag. *"It's Killa"* = signing off. |
| *Killa Season* | Cam's 2006 album / brand era | Now used as a self-reference for the era + style. |
| *Dipset* | the collective + the lifestyle / aesthetic | *"That's Dipset"* = that's the brand. |
| *Byrd Gang* | Jim Jones's offshoot label / collective | Post-2007 Jim Jones brand-extension. |
| *Vampire Life* / *VampLife* | Jim Jones's later brand | Mid-2010s era; nightlife / fashion brand. |
| *730* / *Dips 730* | Freekey Zekey's offshoot | NYC police 730-code (emotionally disturbed) inverted as positive self-tag. |
| *uptown* | Harlem | Self-reference; *"we run uptown"*. |
| *the Avenue* | Lenox Avenue / 7th Avenue / Harlem main streets | Specific block geography. |
| *S.D.E.* | "Sports, Drugs & Entertainment" | Cam'ron's pre-Dipset album / brand statement. |
| *bird* | a kilo of cocaine (street register) | Cam'ron use; gang/street-marked. |
| *no homo* | clarifying-non-homosexual phrase tag | Cam-popularized; widely cited; politically problematic in modern context. **Avoid in customer-facing or LLM-generated content** — flagged as homophobic by GLAAD and broader LGBT advocacy. Historical / academic reference only. |
| *what means the world to you?* | rhetorical Cam'ron self-question | Stage and interview signature. |
| *yeahhh* (drawn-out) | Cam delivery marker | Signature Cam'ron interview / ad-lib. |
| *computers Putin* | "I'm computer Putin / I'm computing" | Signature Cam'ron line; no political reference, just Cam wordplay. |

**Aesthetic / fashion vocabulary** (Dipset normalized as a register
layer):
- *pink* (men's pink wardrobe; Cam wore it on the cover of
  *Purple Haze*, 2004) — used in Dipset register as a fashion-
  signaling word, not a literal color.
- *purple* / *Purple Haze* — Cam's purple-aesthetic era;
  used as brand-marker.
- *fur* — luxury fur coat, signal of status in Dipset register.
- *iced* — iced-out (heavy diamond jewelry).
- *cake* / *cakes* — money / wealth.

**Cadence markers**:
- **Theatrical delivery**: vowels stretched, sentences end in
  rising-then-falling stress for emphasis. Cam'ron in particular
  speaks like a stage performer in conversation.
- **Repetition with intensification**: *"yeah, yeah, yeahhh"* /
  *"Killa, Killa, Killaaa"* as turn-closers.
- **Punchline-first sentence shape**: Cam often leads with the
  signature line, then explains.
- **Comfortable with confident silence**: long pauses where the
  speaker has clearly already won the conversation.

**Operating-register modulation**:
- **Customer-facing** — OFF entirely. Dipset register is heavily
  fashion-coded, gang-adjacent, brand-tied; reads as opaque or
  inappropriate to a mainstream public.
- **Hip-hop cultural / Harlem-rooted character** — appropriate
  when the character's origin justifies it.
- **Internal team / cipher** — Dipset register as a Harlem-rooted
  character's home; can be deployed at full home register.

**Gatekeeping**:
- *Killa* / *Killaaa* sign-off is **Cam-specific** — using it on
  a non-Cam-affiliated Harlem character reads as cosplay.
- *Byrd Gang* / *VampLife* / *730* are direct affiliations —
  only deploy on actual Jim Jones / Freekey Zekey affiliated
  characters.
- *no homo* is **forbidden** in any FOAI-generated content per
  default content policy (homophobic phrase, even when used in
  historical-reference context, requires editorial framing). LLM
  system prompts should explicitly block.

**Anti-patterns**:
- Don't conflate Dipset register with general Harlem register.
  Dipset is a SUB-register; non-Dipset Harlem speakers (Big L,
  Ma$e in his return-to-faith era, A$AP Rocky's distinct register)
  speak Harlem differently.
- Don't deploy *no homo* in any LLM-generated content.
- Don't use Dipset color-coded fashion vocabulary in non-fashion
  contexts.

- Jim Jones — Drink Champs, ALL OUT, Math Hoffa, REAL LATE.

### Staten Island — Park Hill / Stapleton (Shaolin)
- **Cadence**: slow, deliberate, more deeply 5%-Nation-influenced
  than other boroughs (Wu-Tang lineage).
- **Pitch**: adult-male **90-130 Hz** — Queens-adjacent but with a
  more philosophical / scriptural rhythm.
- **Vocabulary markers**: *peace, god*, *seven*, *cipher*,
  *the science of*, *true mathematics*, *born / U / Now Cipher*
  (Supreme Mathematics). Plus the Wu-specific lexicon below.
- **Anchors**: GZA, Ghostface Killah, Raekwon, Method Man,
  Inspectah Deck, RZA. The Wu-Tang Clan documentary recordings
  (Hulu, archive.org public-domain interview clips), Drink Champs
  Wu-Tang episode.

#### Wu-Tang Slang — the Staten Island sub-register

The **Wu-Tang Clan** (1992-present, Staten Island + Brooklyn) built
a distinct register that fused **kung-fu film mythology**, **Five
Percent Nation numerology**, **chess lore**, **mafia mythology**,
**Bible references**, and neighborhood NY slang into a closed
lexical system. RZA was the architect; Raekwon was the in-house
slang expert (per RZA, Raekwon's *Only Built 4 Cuban Linx* "had
the most slang ever in hip-hop").

**Origin**: The Wu took kung-fu movies (especially *Shaolin and
Wu-Tang*, 1983) as their organizing metaphor. The discipline,
swordsmanship, and clan-loyalty themes mapped onto Staten Island
street life. *Wu* / *Wu-Tang* spread as slang for the
ideology-and-style mixture.

**Vocabulary markers**:

| Term | Meaning | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| *Shaolin* / *Shaolin Land* | Staten Island | Wu-coined, now the standard self-reference for SI Black register. |
| *Wu* | the way / the discipline / the family | *"That's that Wu"* = that's authentic, that's the lineage. |
| *the gods* | the Wu collective + the 5%-Nation gods (overlap layer) | Wu members address each other as gods per 5%-Nation tradition. |
| *the killer bees* | Wu-Tang affiliates | From the *36 Chambers* iconography. |
| *sword style* | flow / signature / lyrical attack | Kung-fu metaphor for rap technique. |
| *the 36 chambers* | the Wu universe / the lessons | Kung-fu film reference doubling as 5%-Nation lesson reference. |
| *triumph* | the highest manifestation / arrival | From the song *Triumph*. |
| *C.R.E.A.M.* | Cash Rules Everything Around Me | Coined by Method Man's associate Raider Ruckus; *cream* alone was already neighborhood slang for money (from Tom & Jerry whipped-cream battles) before the acronym. Now genre-wide. |
| *Cuban Linx* / *Cuban Link* | the mafia-coded heritage layer | From Raekwon's album *Only Built 4 Cuban Linx*; Cuban-link gold chain = wealth marker. |
| *Tony Starks* | Ghostface Killah's mafia persona | Iron Man / Ghostface fusion — Wu uses character-naming in interviews. |
| *Lex Diamond* | Raekwon's mafia persona | Used in interview register, not just lyric register. |
| *the cipher* | the conversation / the gathering / the circle | 5%-Nation overlap; Wu deploys it heavily. |
| *the lab* | the studio | Wu-popularized; now genre-wide. |
| *son* / *god* | term of address | NY-wide *son*, but Wu adds *god* (5%-Nation) at higher density than non-Wu speakers. |
| *the slums of Shaolin* | Staten Island street life | Self-reference. |

**Cross-cuts with other Wu-influenced inheritance**:
- **5%-Nation lexicon** — Wu-Tang carries 5%-Nation almost as densely
  as direct Five-Percenters. *Peace, god* / *the science* / *cipher*
  / *the seven* sit naturally in Wu register.
- **Kung-fu film mythology** — *Shaolin*, *the abbot*, *the master*,
  *the technique*, *the chamber* — these are not metaphors, they're
  the operating vocabulary.
- **Mafia mythology** — Tony Starks, Lex Diamond, Cuban Linx,
  Sosa-style references — Wu pulled from *Scarface* / *Goodfellas*
  / *The Godfather* and made the references load-bearing in their
  conversational style.
- **Chess lore** — RZA in particular speaks chess (*the queen*,
  *the gambit*, *the endgame*) in ordinary conversation.
- **Bible references** — Old Testament more than New, used as
  rhetorical / poetic frame.

**Operating-register modulation**:
- **Customer-facing** — OFF entirely. Wu-Tang Slang is heavily
  layered, comprehension-demanding, and reads as in-group code if
  the audience doesn't share the inheritance.
- **Hip-hop / cultural** — appropriate for any character whose
  origin is Wu-affiliated; otherwise reads as cosplay.
- **Internal team / cipher** — a Wu-rooted character can run their
  full home register here. *Peace god*, *the science*, *that's that
  Wu*, *the cipher* surface naturally.

**Gatekeeping**:
The Wu-Tang Clan operates as a brand-and-family — the gatekeeping
on direct affiliation references (*sword style*, *killer bees*,
*the 36 chambers* used as self-reference, *Tony Starks* / *Lex
Diamond* as alter-egos) is NAMING-LEVEL. A non-Wu-affiliated
character should not deploy these as if they were inside the
family. Generic Wu-influenced terms (*shaolin* for SI, *cream*
for money, *the lab* for studio) have spread genre-wide and are
no longer Wu-gated.

## Heritage layers running across boroughs

### 5%-Nation / Nation of Gods and Earths lexicon
The **Five Percent Nation** (Nation of Gods and Earths), founded by
Allah the Father (Clarence 13X) in Harlem in 1964, gave NYC hip-hop
its philosophical-language layer. Even speakers who are not Five-
Percenters often inherit the lexicon through hip-hop transmission.

Core canonical phrases and their function:

| Phrase | Function |
|---|---|
| *peace, god* | greeting between Five-Percenter men ("god" = the Black man as god in microcosm) |
| *peace, earth* | greeting to a woman ("earth" = the Black woman) |
| *true and living* | sincere, genuine, real |
| *cipher* | conversation, gathering, the circle of understanding |
| *build* / *destroy* | constructive talk / destructive talk |
| *the science of* | the deeper logic / inner workings of (anything) |
| *born / U / Now Cipher* | numerical-word tags from Supreme Mathematics (1=Knowledge, 2=Wisdom, 3=Understanding, 4=Culture, 5=Power, 6=Equality, 7=God, 8=Build/Destroy, 9=Born, 0=Cipher) |
| *the 120* | the 120 Lessons (Five-Percenter scripture) |
| *manifest* | reveal / make real |
| *Allah-school* | school of thought rooted in 5% teachings |
| *the seven* | the highest manifestation of god / man |
| *poor righteous* | committed to the truth, not the world's wealth |

**How to apply**: ACHEEVY uses a LIGHT layer of this lexicon — 1-2
phrases per long conversation, never as performance, always as the
natural rhythm of someone raised in the cultural inheritance. Heavy
deployment reads as cosplay.

### Caribbean / West-Indian diaspora prosody
Brooklyn and Bronx Black speech often carries Jamaican, Trinidadian,
Haitian, or Bajan prosody from family origin — even when the speaker
themselves was NYC-born. Markers:
- Rising-falling sentence-end intonation (compare neutral US English's
  falling-only).
- Vowel rounding in *-ow* (*go* → *go-uh*).
- Vocabulary loans (*bredren*, *star*, *yute*, *whagwan*).

### Black-Southern Migration cadence
Older Harlem speakers, older Brooklyn speakers, and most Black NYC
preachers carry the cadence their grandparents brought from
Mississippi / South Carolina / Alabama / Virginia during the Great
Migration. Pulpit register sits on top — see
`URBANISM/subcultural/church.md`.

## Procurement workflow (canonical)

1. Run `research_voice_register.py --speaker "<NAME>" --group URBANISM
   --region east-coast-ny --target acheevy_baritone --max-clips N`.
2. For long-form podcasts, set `--skip-intro 1500` (skip the first
   25 minutes of intro/ads/banter to land in the speaker monologue).
3. Analyzer slides 30-second non-overlapping windows across each
   downloaded clip and reports the best-matching window's features.
4. Download lives in `audio/` with provenance JSON sidecar
   (source URL, ingestion timestamp, license signal).
5. Top-ranked clip is the candidate **register reference**, not the
   cloning source — for cloning we pull rights-cleared owner-recorded
   audio. Reference is for register / cadence tuning of the LLM
   system prompt and TTS voice selection.

## Anti-patterns

- Don't homogenize the boroughs. *"NYC Black speech"* doesn't exist
  as a single thing — it's a constellation of registers.
- Don't deploy 5%-Nation lexicon as flavor on a non-Five-Percenter
  voice. *"Peace, god"* is a greeting between gods; it carries
  meaning. Cosplay-using it is wrong.
- Don't write a Brooklyn voice as if it were Queens (or vice versa).
  The cadence is borough-marked.
- Don't import LA or Atlanta cadence ('valley-Black', 'trap-triplet').
  East Coast NYC has its own thing.
- Don't assume every Black-American voice in NYC carries Caribbean
  prosody — heritage layers are individual, not universal.
