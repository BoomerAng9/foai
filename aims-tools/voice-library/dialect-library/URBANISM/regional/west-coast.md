# URBANISM — West Coast (Los Angeles + Bay Area)

> West Coast Black-American speech is fundamentally different from East
> Coast NY. Where the East Coast is dense, fast, and clipped, the West
> Coast is slow, syllable-stretched, and air-comfortable. The signature
> is the **air around the words** — pauses are longer, vowels are held
> longer, sentence ends fall lower. Sun-baked cadence, not subway
> cadence.
>
> The two main hubs (LA and the Bay) carry distinct registers under the
> same family. Snoop Dogg from Long Beach and E-40 from Vallejo speak
> the same language with different prosody. Each gets its own profile
> below.

## Sub-region 1: Los Angeles

### Compton / Long Beach / South Central / Watts
- **Cadence**: SLOW. Vowel-stretched. Sentence-end pitch falls deeply.
  Comfortable with a 3-4 second pause. The signature G-funk-era
  spoken cadence (parallel to the musical G-funk: laid back, low-end
  heavy).
- **Pitch**: adult-male F0 typically **85-130 Hz** — chesty, low.
  Tilts deeper than NYC NY-borough averages.
- **Phonetic markers**:
  - Heavy monophthongization of *-ay* (*time* → *taahm*, *I* → *aah*).
  - *-or* lowers (*more* → *moh*, *for* → *fuh*).
  - *-ow* extends (*go* → *go-uh*).
  - Rounded *-th* (*they* → *dey*, *this* → *dis* — variable, often
    register-marked).
  - Final *-r* slack (*here* → *hee-uh*).
- **Vocabulary markers**:
  - *cuz* (form of address, *cousin* derivative — neutral, not
    gang-marked).
  - *bruh-bruh* (close friend, intimacy intensifier).
  - *the homie*, *my homie*.
  - *pull up*, *we outside*.
  - *thuggin'*, *bangin'* (gang-life-marked when register is appropriate;
    avoid in mainstream registers).
  - *fa sho*, *fo sho*.
  - *dub* (twenty / round number).
  - *foe-deuce* (.42 / .45 caliber — gang-marked).
  - *Tha set*, *the click*.
  - *real one*, *the realest*.
- **Anchors for ear-training (public-interview audio is fair-use
  reference per `feedback_fair_use_training_policy.md`)**:
  - **Tupac Shakur** — *(Compton-LA / NY-born / Baltimore-raised — but
    his speech moved into West Coast register after relocating)*. PBS
    interviews, the MTV unplugged conversations, Death Row era press,
    *Resurrection* documentary footage.
  - **Snoop Dogg (Calvin Broadus Jr.)** — Long Beach. Drink Champs
    full episode, Joe Rogan, Howard Stern, Master & Dynamic, GQ
    long-form, basically every long-form podcast since 2018.
  - **Kendrick Lamar** — Compton. *DAMN* press tour, NYT podcast,
    Trevor Noah Daily Show.
  - **The Game (Jayceon Taylor)** — Compton. R&B Money Podcast,
    Math Hoffa.
  - **YG (Keenon Jackson)** — Compton/Bompton. Drink Champs.
  - **Nipsey Hussle (Ermias Asghedom)** — Crenshaw. Long Beach Griot,
    Power 106, The Breakfast Club. Eritrean-American, but his West
    Coast cadence is foundational. **Acoustic note**: Nipsey carries
    measured pulpit cadence within the LA register (the *build /
    pause / land*) — he's the West Coast example of the cross-cut
    described in `URBANISM/subcultural/church.md`.

### Crenshaw district / Hyde Park / Inglewood
- Distinct from Compton/LB by being slightly more middle-class
  in average register — vocabulary skews professional more often,
  AAVE 1-3 with strong code-switch range.
- **Anchors**: Issa Rae (Inglewood, but Crenshaw-adjacent in voice),
  Nipsey Hussle (Crenshaw), Dom Kennedy (Leimert Park).

### LA hip-hop generation — current
- **AAVE intensity**: 3-5. Vocabulary blends West Coast, hip-hop
  national, and TikTok / Internet-loanwords.
- **Anchors**: Roddy Ricch (Compton), Blueface (Mid-City), Drakeo the
  Ruler (LA — slang-heavy), Mustard (LA producer).

## Sub-region 2: Bay Area

### Oakland / Richmond / East Bay
- **Cadence**: faster than LA, but still slower than NYC. Distinctive
  RHYTHMIC EMPHASIS PATTERNS — heavy stress on the second-to-last
  syllable of multi-syllable words (signature Bay rhythm).
- **Pitch**: adult-male F0 typically **90-140 Hz**.
- **Phonetic markers**:
  - Vowel raising in stressed syllables (*man* → *meeun*).
  - *-ing* → *-een* (signature: *hyphy* not *hyphin*; *poppin'* sounds
    closer to *poppeen*).
  - Long *-ee* on stressed syllables (*see* → *seee*).
  - Less monophthongization than LA — Bay keeps the *-ay* diphthong
    more intact.
- **Vocabulary**: see the **Hyphy lexicon** section below.

#### Hyphy — the Bay Area sub-register

The **Hyphy movement** (Vallejo + Oakland, late-1990s through
mid-2000s, ongoing as cultural inheritance) is the Bay's
canonical Black-American hip-hop subculture: high-energy,
fast-paced, slang-dense, with a fashion / dance / car-culture
layer that's tightly integrated with the verbal register.

**Origin**: emerged late-1990s in Oakland and Vallejo. **Mac Dre**
(Andre Hicks, Vallejo, 1970-2004) was the foundational figure;
he founded **Thizz Entertainment** as the movement's record
label. **E-40** (Earl Stevens, Vallejo) is the Hyphy lexicographer
— half the Bay-specific vocabulary either originated with E-40 or
was popularized by him on wax. **Keak Da Sneak** (Oakland)
coined *yadadamean* and *ghost ride the whip*. **Too \$hort**
(Oakland) carries the older-generation Bay register that
predates the Hyphy label.

**Vocabulary markers**:

| Term | Meaning | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| *hyphy* | hyperactive / wild / let-loose | The movement's name; from *hyperactive*. Adjective and lifestyle marker. *"It's hyphy out here."* |
| *yadadamean* / *ya da da mean?* | "do you know what I mean?" | Coined / popularized by Keak Da Sneak. The single most Bay-specific lexical marker. |
| *thizz* | MDMA / ecstasy | Mac Dre's signature; Thizz Entertainment is the label. *"Thizzin'"* = on ecstasy. |
| *thizz face* | the contorted dance-face you make on thizz | Dance signature. |
| *scraper* | American sedan with rims / pipes / stereo | Caddy, Buick, Chevy with the angled-edge body and hyphy-era modifications. |
| *ghost ride the whip* | get out of a moving car and dance alongside it | Keak Da Sneak coined; signature Hyphy-era stunt and meme. |
| *go dumb* / *get dumb* / *going dumb* | let loose energetically | Movement-marked verb. |
| *stunna shades* | oversized sunglasses | Hyphy fashion marker. |
| *hella* | very / a lot | NorCal generic but Bay-density-marked. *"Hella scrapers."* |
| *tight* | good | Bay-marked positive. |
| *fasho* / *fa sho* | for sure | (Shared with LA but Bay also.) |
| *the town* | Oakland | Self-reference. |
| *the V* | Vallejo | Self-reference. |
| *the Bay* | the Bay Area | Catch-all. |
| *thizzelle* | a thizz-pill / a thizz-night | Mac Dre coinage. |
| *pimpin'* | casual term of address among Bay men | NOT pejorative in local register; used like *brother* / *cuz* in other registers. Read carefully outside Bay. |
| *swang* / *swangin'* | drive a scraper expressively | Bay car-culture verb. |
| *Cuttin' Up* | E-40 song; verb means partying / acting out | E-40 vocabulary contribution. |
| *captain save 'em* | a man who pays women to keep them around | E-40 coinage from his 1994 song *Captain Save a Hoe*. |

**Cadence / phonetic signatures** (specific to Hyphy):
- **Rapid clip with stretched stressed syllables** — the Bay's
  rhythmic-emphasis pattern is its signature. Speakers move FAST
  through unstressed words, then HOLD stressed syllables.
- **High-energy intonation** — even in conversational register,
  Hyphy speakers carry an animation that LA Black speech
  typically doesn't.
- **Cross-talk-comfortable** — Hyphy register tolerates and even
  encourages overlapping speech in group settings.

**Operating-register modulation**:
- **Customer-facing** — Hyphy register is OFF on customer
  surfaces. *Yadadamean* / *thizz* / *scraper* / *ghost ride*
  read as opaque to mainstream comprehension.
- **Bay-rooted character internal / friend / culture surface**
  — appropriate for a Bay-rooted character's home register at
  intensity 3-4.
- **Hip-hop cultural surface** — appropriate when the character
  is Bay-affiliated.

**Gatekeeping**:
- *Thizz* / *thizzin'* / *thizzelle* are **Mac-Dre-affiliated**
  brand vocabulary. Non-Bay characters using them reads as
  cosplay.
- *Yadadamean* is now widely-spread (1.5+ decades since coining)
  and is genre-recognized but still strongly Bay-marked.
- *Ghost ride the whip* is so specifically Bay-rooted that any
  character outside the Bay using it triggers code-switch noise.

**Anti-patterns**:
- Don't blend Hyphy lexicon with LA lexicon in a single
  character — the two are distinct and the contrast is
  meaningful.
- Don't deploy *thizz* / drug-marked vocabulary on
  customer-facing or under-21 surfaces.
- Don't use *captain save 'em* / *captain save a [w-word]* —
  the underlying source has misogynist framing; flagged.
- Don't write Hyphy register at LA tempo — the speed and
  rhythmic emphasis IS the register.

## Heritage layers running across West Coast

### Black migration to LA / SF
The Great Migration brought Texas, Louisiana, Mississippi, and
Arkansas Black families to LA and the Bay during WWII (defense
industry jobs in Long Beach shipyards, Oakland docks, Hunters
Point Naval Shipyard). The result:

- LA Black register inherits **Texas-Louisiana cadence** (slow,
  syllable-stretched, vowel-extended). This is why LA Black speech
  sounds slower than NYC speech — the underlying migration register
  is southern slow-drag, plus the additional sun-baked compression
  of LA car-culture isolation.
- Bay Area Black register inherits **Arkansas-Texas cadence** with
  Pacific-Northwest second-generation acceleration. The result is
  more rhythmic syncopation than LA.

### Mexican-American / Chicano / Latino prosody influence
LA Black speech runs alongside Chicano English and bilingual
Mexican-American English — the contact has produced shared vowel
features and shared vocabulary (*cholo*, *vato*, *pendejo* surface
in some LA Black registers as cross-cultural loans).

### Pulpit / faith register
Strong on the West Coast; LA Black church culture (FAME / West
Angeles COGIC / Crenshaw Christian Center) has produced major
pastoral voices that cross into hip-hop and political oratory:
- Bishop Charles Blake (West Angeles COGIC, LA).
- Frederick Price (Crenshaw Christian Center, deceased).
- Bishop Kenneth Ulmer (Faithful Central, Inglewood).
See `URBANISM/subcultural/church.md` for cadence details — the
pulpit register surfaces in Nipsey's interview cadence, Tupac's
late-career interview cadence, and (cross-cut) Kendrick Lamar's
performance cadence.

## Acoustic profile — West Coast adult-male reference target

A target acoustic profile for the LA / Compton / Long Beach
register can be added to `scripts/analyze_voice.py:TARGET_PROFILES`
under name `west_coast_la_baritone`. Suggested values:

```python
"west_coast_la_baritone": {
    "f0_hz_mean":              {"target": 100.0, "tolerance": 25.0, "weight": 3.0},
    "f0_hz_median":            {"target": 95.0, "tolerance": 25.0, "weight": 2.0},
    "spectral_centroid_hz":    {"target": 1600.0, "tolerance": 700.0, "weight": 2.0},
    "speech_rate_syllables_per_sec": {"target": 2.8, "tolerance": 1.0, "weight": 2.0},  # SLOWER than NYC
    "hnr_db":                  {"target": 11.0, "tolerance": 5.0, "weight": 1.5},
    "voiced_fraction":         {"target": 0.55, "tolerance": 0.2, "weight": 1.0},
}
```

The West Coast target differs from `acheevy_baritone` (East-Coast NY
reference) primarily in:
- Slightly LOWER F0 mean (100 vs 105 Hz) — LA chest register tilts
  deeper.
- Slightly LOWER spectral centroid (1600 vs 1700 Hz) — warmer / less
  bright.
- SLOWER speech rate (2.8 vs 3.2 sps) — vowel-stretched cadence.

## When to assign this register

- Any Coastal or FOAI character set in / from LA, Long Beach,
  Compton, Inglewood, Crenshaw, the South Bay.
- Bay Area characters get the `bay_area` sub-tag (use the same
  group/region but tag the sub-region in the spec).
- Hip-hop characters whose acoustic anchor is West-Coast-rooted
  (any of the listed anchors above).
- NOT Coastal Brewing's primary cast — Coastal lives in Bluffton/
  Beaufort/Savannah. Use this register only for special-guest
  characters or future verticals.

## Anti-patterns

- Don't blend LA + Bay register markers in a single character. They
  are distinct.
- Don't import East Coast NY markers (*deadass*, *son*, *no doubt*,
  *wild'in*) into a West Coast voice.
- Don't deploy gang-marked vocabulary (*bangin'*, *foe-deuce*,
  *the set*) on a customer-facing surface — the register-modulation
  doctrine (REGISTER-MODULATION.md) gates these out at any operating
  register where the audience is mainstream public.
- Don't write LA register at NYC tempo. The slowness IS the
  register; speed-it-up reads as inauthentic.
- Don't perform Snoop's specific phonemic style as "West Coast Black
  voice" — Snoop is a STYLED variant, not the median. Use Kendrick
  Lamar / Issa Rae / Nipsey conversational footage as the median
  reference; Snoop is one possible point in the range.
