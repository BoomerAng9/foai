# Letter of Intent — Reusable Template

A.I.M.S. canonical LOI template, distilled from real ACHIEVEMOR partnership letters (CDL BookClub 2023, Rider Academy 2023). Use this as a starting point; reference the example outputs in `examples/loi/` for tone and voice.

---

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR TITLE]
[YOUR COMPANY]

[DATE]

[RECIPIENT NAME]
[RECIPIENT COMPANY]

Re: Letter of Intent — [PARTNERSHIP TITLE]

Dear [RECIPIENT NAME],

As the main representative of [YOUR COMPANY], I am writing to express our interest in exploring collaboration with [RECIPIENT COMPANY]. This Letter of Intent outlines our proposal for [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF PROPOSED COLLABORATION] (the "Proposed Collaboration"). Please note that this letter contains non-binding provisions of understanding between the parties and does not impose any legal obligations on either party, except as explicitly stated.

Our proposal is as follows:

## 1. PROPOSED COLLABORATION

[YOUR COMPANY] will [DESCRIBE WHAT YOU WILL DO — investments, deliverables, support]. [RECIPIENT COMPANY] will [DESCRIBE WHAT THEY WILL DO]. [INCLUDE COMPENSATION DETAILS, ALLOWANCES, COMMISSIONS, NEGOTIATION TERMS].

## 2. TIMING

The parties shall execute the Final Agreements and close the Proposed Transaction by [CLOSING DATE] (the "Closing Date"), subject to extension as discussed in Section 6 below.

## 3. CONTINGENCIES

Any obligation to consummate the Proposed Transaction is based entirely on satisfaction of the following conditions:
(a) Receipt of all applicable consents, approvals, and authorizations including board, partnership, third-party, and regulatory approvals
(b) Completion by each party of substantial due diligence investigation of all relevant business, legal, financial, and operational documents, with results satisfactory to that party, no later than [DUE DILIGENCE DATE] (the "Due Diligence Completion Date")

## 4. DUE DILIGENCE

While this letter remains in effect, each party and its advisors shall have reasonable access to the other party's books, records, and personnel files, and shall receive such financial and operational data and other information as that party may reasonably request. Any received information will be kept confidential by the receiving party. On termination or expiration of this letter, each party shall return all printed information received from the other party in connection with the Proposed Transaction.

## 5. NOTICE AFTER COMPLETION OF DUE DILIGENCE

On or before the Due Diligence Completion Date, each party shall notify the other party in writing that it has completed substantial due diligence and is prepared to proceed with consummation of the Proposed Transaction (the "Notice of Intention to Proceed"). If a party does not provide a Notice of Intention to Proceed by the Due Diligence Completion Date, the other party may cancel this proposal and neither party will have any obligation or liability to the other party.

## 6. EXTENSION OF TIME FOR CLOSING

If the Proposed Transaction is not completed by the Closing Date and each party has been operating in good faith to complete its due diligence and negotiate the transaction documents, the parties shall evaluate progress toward closing and, if suitable progress is being made, discuss in good faith a revised Closing Date. If the parties have not made satisfactory progress toward closing, either party may withdraw from the Proposed Transaction without further obligation or liability.

## 7. FINAL AGREEMENTS

The Final Agreements may include customary covenants, conditions, representations, and warranties. As this is a nonbinding letter, additional elements may be subject to negotiation and inclusion.

## 8. EXCLUSIVE DEALING

In consideration of the effort and expense to be incurred by the parties in connection with their due diligence review, for a period of [EXCLUSIVITY DAYS] days from the date of this letter, neither party or its officers, directors, employees, and agents may initiate, encourage, or accept any third-party offer or proposal related to the Proposed Transaction.

## 9. EXPENSES

Each party is responsible for all of its own costs and expenses incurred in connection with due diligence related to the request for services, whether or not the Proposed Transaction is consummated. Except for breach of any confidentiality provisions, neither party will have any liability to the other party for any expenses incurred if negotiations are terminated.

## 10. NONBINDING LETTER; WITHDRAWAL

Except for the provisions set forth in Section 1 (Proposed Collaboration), this letter as a whole does not constitute a legal and binding obligation or agreement between the parties. Either party may unilaterally withdraw from negotiation at any time for any reason at the withdrawing party's sole discretion by notifying the other party in writing.

If the terms of this letter are acceptable to you, please sign in the space provided below and return it to the attention of the undersigned no later than [SIGN-BY DATE].

Signed,

[YOUR COMPANY]
By: [YOUR NAME], [YOUR TITLE]

[RECIPIENT COMPANY]
By: [RECIPIENT NAME], [RECIPIENT TITLE]

## Generation guidance for ACHEEVY/Boomer_CFO

When generating an LOI for a user partnership:
1. Read both example outputs (`examples/loi/cdl-bookclub-2023.md`, `examples/loi/rider-academy-2023.md`) for tone — warm but professional, urgency without pressure
2. Always lead with intent + acknowledgment that the letter is non-binding
3. Always preserve the 10-section structure (renumber if combining)
4. Always preserve the dual-signature block at the bottom
5. Match the voice: confident, partnership-oriented, not transactional
